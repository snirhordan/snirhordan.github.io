Technion, Israel Institute of Technology
Faculty of Mathematics
Supervised by Asst. Prof. Nadav Dym

I research machine learning models that process geometric data such as graphs and point clouds. My work focuses on two interlocking paradigms: identifying the shortcomings of geometric models and developing geometrically complete models, favorably with low-dimensional features, yielding more powerful architectures with moderate computational cost.

Research area: Geometric Deep Learning

Publications

Spectral Graph Neural Networks are Incomplete on Graphs with a Simple Spectrum

S Hordan, M Bechler-Speicher, G Lifshitz, N Dym

Conference on Neural Information Processing Systems (NeurIPS), 2025 Spotlight, top 3%

arXiv Code
We prove that spectral graph neural networks are incomplete on graphs with a simple spectrum, establishing fundamental expressiveness limitations of spectral GNN architectures. We further propose a novel spectral GNN architecture that overcomes these limitations.

Weisfeiler Leman for Euclidean Equivariant Machine Learning

S Hordan, T Amir, N Dym

International Conference on Machine Learning (ICML), 2024

arXiv Code ICML 2024
We construct the first universal and equivariant 3D point cloud network with polynomial complexity, solving a key open problem in the field. We achieve state-of-the-art results on molecular conformation generation benchmarks.

Complete Neural Networks for Euclidean Graphs

S Hordan, T Amir, SJ Gortler, N Dym

AAAI Conference on Artificial Intelligence (AAAI), 2024

arXiv AAAI 2024
We construct a universal invariant 3D point cloud network with polynomial time complexity. The architecture is provably complete for Euclidean graphs, developed in collaboration with Harvard University.
